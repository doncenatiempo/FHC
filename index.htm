Class Description
Title - Level Description
Introduction to BYU's Record Linking Lab - Intermediate Come and see how the Record Linking Lab is developing and using powerful technology to increase the quality and coverage of the Family Tree on FamilySearch. You will see a demonstration of volunteer resources that are easy to use, such as automated indexing and customized record hints, that will help you and other people build the Family Tree, as well as find relatives who need temple ordinances performed.
How to Find and Enhance Photos of Ancestors - Intermediate This class will help find possible photos of ancestors who you have no photos of... at minimum a headstone photo or other photos you are not aware of that might exist on Find a Grave. Also, how to find possible school yearbook photos using Ancestry.com. Then take these photos or any photo that you would like to enhance by making the photo clearer, repair scratches, change photos to color using the tools from MyHeritage.com.
Using TribalPage.com to help you build, maintain and share your Family Tree online - All Simple and easy to use charts and reports, mapping and free newsletters customized for your family. A unique way to preserve your genealogical discoveries, while at the same time sharing them with an entire family. You can also use this TribalPage.com to create your own personal family history book, or as a launching pad for missionary work.
Swedish Ancestry - Intermediate Class canceled.
How to Get Started With FamilySeach - Beginner If you are new to family history and want to know how to get started using FamilySearch, this class is for you.
Introduction to Goldie May, a fascinating program to serve you and your fellow indexers - Intermediate Learn of a new concept in furthering family data collection by taking US Censuses information and potentially matching it to FamilySearch families. Censuses have been previously indexed, but not yet matched to the individuals in FamilySearch. All records are typed making them easy to read.
Family History Fun For Families - All We will discuss ways to have fun with family history and how to incorporate it into every day life. This is applicable whether you have kids, grandkids, teenagers, or no kids. It's bringing names and dates to life.
Card System or "An Interview with Your Deceased Ancestors" - Intermediate Look deeper to find possible ways to discover names that are missing for your family.
I Took A DNA Test, Now What? - Beginner / Intermediate How to interpret your DNA results to find possible ways to further your family line.
Take A Name App - Beginner Going to the temple? Learn how to use the 'Take A Name' app on your mobile devices.
FamilySearch Activities - Beginner How to use FamilySearch's family activities in a way that the whole family of any age can engage and discover creative ways to learn about their family history.
Interview Questions and Attaching Voice Recordings - Beginner Attaching voice recordings to FamilySearch and questions to ask in an interview.
Non-traditional Family Trees in FamilySearch - Beginner Learn how to edit your FamilySearch tree to show non-traditional families including: Step parents & children, same gender couples, etc.
Languages Indexing & Reviewing - Intermediate / Advanced This class will focus on indexing in other languages with emphasis on Romance languages. Also covered are: cultural norms, calligraphy, guides, method sources and other helps.
An introduction to Ancestry.com - Beginner / Intermediate A basic introduction to Ancestry.com. Start a new tree, using the green leaf hints. How to use time lines and gallery photos.
FamilySearch Memories App - Beginner Learn how to attach documents such as photos and histories into FamilySearch.
How to Index - Beginner To index, you type in information from historical documents so they can be published and become searchable online. Come learn how to participate in this vital effort to help people find their family members.
FamilySearch Records - Behind the Scenes Class canceled.
FamilySearch and Standardizing Data Entries - All This class will demonstrate how to properly standardize names, dates, and places on the FamilySearch website. Using standard formats for names, dates and places in Family Tree improves the accuracy and searchability of the information you enter.
FamilySearch Partner Site's Overview - Intermediate An oral review with demonstration of the FamilySearch partner sites, including history and advantages of each site.
