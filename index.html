Teacher Class Title Level Short Description
1 Lisa Gardner Introduction to BYU's Record Linking Lab I Come and see how the Record Linking Lab is developing and using powerful technology to increase the quality and coverage of the Family Tree on FamilySearch. You will see a demonstration of volunteer resources that are easy to use, such as automated indexing and customized record hints, that will help you and other people build the Family Tree, as well as find relatives who need temple ordinances performed.
2 Mark Gardner How to Find and Enhance Photos of Ancestors I This class will help find possible photos of ancestors who you have no photos of... at minimum a headstone photo or other photos you are not aware of that might exist on Find a Grave.  Also, how to find possible school yearbook photos using Ancestry.com.  Then take these photos or any photo that you would like to enhance by making the photo clearer, repair scratches, change photos to color using the tools from MyHeritage.com.
3 Scott Duncan Using TribalPage.com to help you build, maintain and share your Family Tree online All Simple and easy to use charts and reports, mapping and free newsletters customized for your family. A unique way to preserve your genealogical discoveries, while at the same time sharing them with an entire family. You can also use this TribalPage.com to create your own personal family history book, or as a launching pad for missionary work.
4 Anna Wilder Swedish Ancestry I An overview of Swedish research. The records currently available on family search and my heritage. As well as what's available at the family history center. Also the Swedish Genealogical Word List in the family search wiki.
5 Patrick Wilder How to Get Started With FamilySeach B If you are new to family history and want to know how to get started using FamilySearch, this class is for you.
6 Marc Ellsworth Introduction to Goldie May, a fascinating program to serve you and your fellow indexers I Learn of a new concept in furthering family data collection by taking US Censuses information and potentially matching it to FamilySearch families. Censuses have been previously indexed, but not yet matched to the individuals in FamilySearch. All records are typed making them easy to read.
7 Tiffany Applegate Family History Fun For Families All We will discuss ways to have fun with family history and how to incorporate it into every day life. This is applicable whether you have kids, grandkids, teenagers, or no kids.  It's bringing names and dates to life.
8 Anna Price Card System or "An Interview with Your Deceased Ancestors" I Look deeper to find possible ways to discover work that is missing for your family.
9 Melissa Lopez I Took A DNA Test, Now What? B & I How to interpret your DNA results to find possible ways to further your family line.
10 Ruth Pargeter Take A Name App B Going to the temple?  Learn how to use the 'Take A Name' app on your mobile devices.
11 Camila & Rebeca Lopez FamilySearch Activities B How to use FamilySearch's family activities in a way that the whole family of any age can engage and discover creative ways to learn about their family history.
12 Bishop Lopez & Deacons Interview Questions and Attaching Voice Recordings B Attaching voice recordings to FamilySearch and questions to ask in an interview.
13 Elenna Chipman Non-traditional Family Trees in FamilySearch B Learn how to edit your FamilySearch tree to show non-traditional famiilies including: Step parents & children, same gender couples, etc.
14 Royce Peterson Languages Indexing & Reviewing I & A This class will focus on indexing in other languages with emphasis on Romance languages.  Also covered are: cultural norms, caligraphy, guides, method sources and other helps.
15 Don Holm An introduction to Ancestry.com B & I A basic introduction to Ancestry.com. Start a new tree, using the green leaf hints. How to use time lines and gallery photos.
16 Kerry Finlinson FamilySearch Memories App B Learn how to attach documents such as photos and histories into FamilySearch.
17 Lori Layton How to Index B To index, you type in information from historical documents so they can be published and become searchable online. Come learn how to participate in this vital effort to help people find their family members.
18 Cathy & Terry Lambson FamilySearch Records - Behind the Scenes All How FamilySearch, with the support of Church Service Missionaries,  finds and prepares record for Indexing.
19 Claudia Gesell FamilySearch - Standardizing Data Entries All This class will demonstrate how to properly standardize names, dates, and places on the FamilySearch website.  Using standard formats for names, dates and places in Family Tree improves the accuracy and searchability of the information you enter.
20 Larry Gesell FamilySearch Partner Site's Overview I An oral review with demonstration of the FamilySearch partner sites, including history and advantages of each site.
